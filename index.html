About FarmDrop

Our Mission

FarmDrop is a Maine-based, mission-driven collaborative marketplace. We host multiple farmers and producers together on a local online storefront, making it easy for communities to access fresh, locally-grown food year-round.

Our advance-pay model ensures farmers are paid as soon as customers place orders — not after delivery. This sustains a system where farmers only bring what they've sold, reducing waste and guaranteeing income.

Leadership

Hannah Semler, CEO — A Blue Hill native who spent years living in Spain and Central America, Hannah has long been both food and farm obsessed. She and her then business partner took over FarmDrop and grew it into the statewide platform it is today, supporting 65+ farmers and over 1,000 customers across Maine.

How the Marketplace Works

Weekly Online Shopping

Customers shop each week for produce, meat, dairy, coffee, soap, pre-made meals, and more — all sourced from local Maine farms.

Advance Payment to Farmers

Unlike farmers markets or retail stores, farmers are paid the moment an order is placed. This financial model sustains small farms.

Market Hub Managers

Each location has a dedicated Market Hub Manager who receives bulk harvest deliveries and parcels out individual customer orders for pickup.

Local Food Coordinator Jobs

FarmDrop provides gig-economy local food coordinator positions, with plans to add dozens more jobs across the state.

Food for Health Program

About the Program

Food for Health formally launched in 2025 in response to increasing food access challenges following reductions in federal programs such as SNAP.

The program connects Maine communities facing food insecurity with fresh, locally-grown produce and products through FarmDrop's existing distribution network.

How It Works

Existing Distribution Routes

Food security organizations receive deliveries along FarmDrop's established routes — no extra logistics needed.

Restaurant Partnerships

In 2025, FarmDrop also launched initiatives to support restaurants sourcing from local farms, expanding demand for farmer products.

Farmer Participation

As a farmer, you can opt into Food for Health by marking products available at reduced or subsidized pricing for qualifying organizations.
